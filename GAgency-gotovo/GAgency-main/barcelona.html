Barcelona

Barcelona is a city, seaport, and the capital of the Barcelona province and Catalonia autonomous community in northeastern Spain, situated 90 miles (150 km) south of the French border. Known for its unique character, cultural significance, and stunning physical appearance, it is the major commercial center and Mediterranean port of Spain.

In 1862, Hans Christian Andersen famously called Barcelona the "Paris of Spain" during his visit. The city is an important cultural hub with a rich history, boasting of numerous archives, libraries, museums, and buildings with impressive examples of modernist and Art Nouveau decor and architecture. With the official recognition of the Catalan language and substantial levels of regional self-governance since the late 1970s, the city's cultural life has been revitalized, highlighting the depth and variety of Catalan culture. The vibrant cultural scene is enhanced by Barcelona's striking natural setting, situated between scenic mountains and the Mediterranean Sea with a pleasant climate that encourages outdoor life, and its significance as an economic powerhouse and major port, making it a city of infinite possibilities. The city covers an area of 38 square miles (98 square km), while its metropolitan area extends to 1,249 square miles (3,235 square km).
